Oculoplastics surgeon, American board-certified ophthalmologist

Pimkwan Jaru-ampornpan is an American-board certified ophthalmologist who specializes in oculoplastics and reconstructive surgery. She graduated with academic distinction from Harvard College and began her medical training at the Vanderbilt University School of Medicine. She then completed a residency in ophthalmology at University of Wisconsin, and sub-specialized fellowship in Orbital Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery at the Kellogg Eye Center, University of Michigan. After 15 years of medical training in the US, she returned to her homeland and is currently a clinical faculty at the Department of Ophthalmology, Faculty of Medicine Siriraj Hospital, Mahidol University, Bangkok, Thailand. Given that Siriraj hospital is considered as one of the top quaternary-care medical centers in Thailand, Pimkwan treats patients with eyelid, orbital and lacrimal problems from all over Thailand and its neighboring countries. She does clinical research in ophthalmology with a focus on oculoplastics, orbital and lacrimal disorders. Currently, she also holds a position as a director of SIriraj Ocular Prosthetic Center.
